# Performance Scoring

The performance score is a composite metric that measures an employee's attendance behavior over time. It is updated on a rolling basis and serves as both a personal indicator and an input to the system's machine learning models.

---

## Score Components

The score is built from five factors, each weighted and normalized before being combined into the final score (0–100).

| Factor | What It Measures | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Attendance Rate** | Percentage of working days the employee was present (Present or Late) | High |
| **Work Hours** | Average daily work hours compared to the expected minimum | High |
| **Late Login Count** | Frequency of late check-ins within the scoring window | Medium |
| **Idle Time** | Total idle periods during sessions, relative to session duration | Medium |
| **Overtime Frequency** | How often the employee's sessions extend beyond standard hours | Low (positive) |

> Overtime is a positive contributor — it reflects additional time committed. It does not offset other negative factors but adds to the overall score.

---

## Score Calculation

Each factor is normalized to a 0–1 scale using Min-Max scaling, then combined using configured weights. The final score is mapped to a 0–100 range.

**Simplified formula:**

```
Score = (w1 × Attendance Rate) + (w2 × Work Hour Score) 
      + (w3 × Late Penalty) + (w4 × Idle Penalty) 
      + (w5 × Overtime Bonus)
```

Where `w1` through `w5` are weights that sum to 1.0. The default weight configuration prioritizes Attendance Rate and Work Hours.

---

## Scoring Window

| Setting | Default |
|---|---|
| **Calculation frequency** | Daily (recalculated each night) |
| **Rolling window** | 30-day rolling average |
| **Display on dashboard** | Current score + 30-day trend sparkline |

The 30-day rolling window means the score responds gradually to behavioral changes — a single missed day does not immediately collapse the score, and consistent improvement is reflected over 2–4 weeks.

---

## Score Range and Classification

| Score Range | Classification | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 71 – 100 | **High Performer** | Consistent attendance, meets or exceeds work hour expectations |
| 41 – 70 | **Average** | Generally consistent with occasional deviations |
| 0 – 40 | **At Risk** | Irregular attendance patterns requiring attention |

The classification is displayed on:
- The employee's personal dashboard.
- The manager's team performance view.
- The Employee Classification ML model output.

---

## Score Breakdown and Explainability

Each employee can view a breakdown of their score by clicking **Score Breakdown** on the dashboard.

The breakdown shows:

1. **Factor-level contribution** — How many points each of the five factors added or subtracted.
2. **SHAP-based explanation** — A plain-language summary of which factors had the greatest influence on the score.

**Example breakdown display:**

> Your score this month is **68 / 100 (Average).**
>
> - Attendance Rate contributed positively (+38 pts) — you attended 92% of working days.
> - Work Hours contributed positively (+24 pts) — average of 7.8 hrs/day.
> - Late Login Count reduced your score (−10 pts) — 6 late logins this month.
> - Idle Time had a minor negative impact (−4 pts).

---

## How Scores Feed Into ML Models

The performance score is used as:

- **Input feature** for the Performance Prediction model (XGBoost), which forecasts future scores.
- **Label** for the Employee Classification model (Logistic Regression), which classifies employees into performance tiers.
- **Signal** for the Anomaly Detection model, where a sudden drop in score triggers further investigation.

See [Predictive Analytics](./predictive-analytics.md) for details on how scores are used in forecasting.
