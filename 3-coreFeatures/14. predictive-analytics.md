# Predictive Analytics

The predictive analytics layer uses machine learning models to forecast future attendance trends, estimate performance scores, and classify employee risk levels. These insights help managers and HR teams make proactive workforce decisions.

---

## What the System Forecasts

| Prediction | Who It's For | Where It Appears |
|---|---|---|
| **Performance Score Forecast** | Employee, Manager | Employee dashboard, Manager reports |
| **Employee Classification** | Manager, HR | Manager dashboard, Classification report |
| **Attendance Trend Forecast** | Manager, HR | Manager dashboard, Forecast panel |
| **Absenteeism Risk** | Manager, HR | Predictive reports, Chatbot |

---

## Performance Score Prediction

**Model:** XGBoost Regression

The model analyzes an employee's behavioral history and estimates how their performance score is likely to trend over the next 30 days.

### Input Features Used
- Attendance rate (last 30 and 90 days)
- Average daily work hours
- Late login frequency
- Idle time ratio
- Overtime frequency
- Attendance consistency score
- Login time deviation

### Output
A predicted performance score for each of the next 30 days, displayed as a trend line on the employee's dashboard and in manager reports.

### Reading the Forecast

| Trend | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Upward trend | Behavioral improvement — score expected to rise |
| Flat trend | Stable behavior — score unlikely to change significantly |
| Downward trend | Deteriorating patterns — may warrant a check-in with the employee |

The forecast chart shows a **confidence band** around the predicted line. A wider band indicates more uncertainty in the prediction, typically due to limited recent data.

## Employee Classification

**Model:** Logistic Regression

Each employee is assigned to one of three categories based on their attendance patterns:

| Classification | Description |
|---|---|
| **Consistent** | Attendance is regular, predictable, and within expected parameters |
| **Irregular** | Attendance shows variability — frequent late logins, occasional absences, or inconsistent hours |
| **High-Risk** | Attendance patterns suggest a significant risk of future absenteeism or continued underperformance |

### How Classifications Are Used
- Visible to managers in the team performance dashboard.
- Used as an input feature in the attendance trend forecast.
- High-Risk classifications trigger a recommendation in the manager's alert panel to schedule a check-in with the employee.

Classifications update with each model retraining cycle (monthly by default).

---

## Attendance Trend Forecasting

**Model:** Time-Series (ARIMA / Prophet)

The attendance trend model forecasts expected team attendance levels for the next 7 days, helping managers plan for coverage gaps before they occur.

### Output
- Predicted headcount (number of employees expected to be present) per day for the next 7 days.
- Displayed in the **Forecast Panel** on the manager dashboard.
- Exportable from **Reports → Predictive Analytics → Attendance Forecast**.

### Reading the Forecast

| Indicator | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Green band | Expected attendance within normal range |
| Yellow band | Moderate shortfall predicted — below average but manageable |
| Red band | Significant attendance shortfall predicted — proactive action recommended |

---

## Absenteeism Risk

The system combines classification, trend, and behavioral signals to generate a per-employee absenteeism risk score for the next 7–14 days.

Employees flagged as **High Risk for Absenteeism** appear in:
- **Reports → Predictive Analytics → Absenteeism Risk**.
- Chatbot responses to "Which employees are at risk of absenteeism next week?"

This is intended as an early-warning signal, not a disciplinary indicator. Managers are encouraged to use it as a prompt for proactive engagement rather than a basis for punitive action.

---

## Model Retraining and Data Freshness

Predictive models are retrained on a schedule to reflect the latest data:

| Model | Default Retraining Frequency |
|---|---|
| Performance Prediction (XGBoost) | Monthly |
| Employee Classification (Logistic Regression) | Monthly |
| Attendance Trend Forecast (Time-Series) | Weekly |

A model trained more than 2 weeks ago relative to the current date will display a **stale model** warning banner on the relevant report. Admins can trigger a manual retraining job from the Admin Console. See [Admin User Guide](../user-guides/admin-user-guide.md).

---

## Limitations

- Predictions are probabilistic estimates, not guarantees. They should inform decisions, not replace judgment.
- Forecast accuracy decreases for employees with fewer than 30 days of attendance history in the system.
- Trend forecasts do not account for unplanned events (public holidays added after the model was trained, organizational changes, etc.).
- Classifications reflect behavioral patterns, not intent or personal circumstances.
